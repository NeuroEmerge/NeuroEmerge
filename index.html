01 / About

Where Neuroscience Meets Computation

NeuroEmerge is a research collective dedicated to uncovering the fundamental principles that give rise to intelligent behavior in both biological brains and artificial neural networks.

Our work spans computational neuroscience, emergent systems theory, and foundation model interpretability. We seek unified mathematical frameworks that explain how complex cognitive functions emerge from simple local rules.

02 / Research Areas

Core Research Directions

Neural Network Dynamics

Studying how spatiotemporal patterns of activity emerge in recurrent and feedforward neural architectures, both biological and artificial.

Dynamical Systems Recurrent Nets

Emergent Intelligence

Investigating phase transitions in model scaling, where capabilities spontaneously appear beyond critical parameter thresholds.

Scaling Laws Phase Transitions

Biologically Plausible Learning

Developing learning algorithms consistent with known neurobiology, including Hebbian plasticity and credit assignment mechanisms.

Hebbian Theory Plasticity

Model Interpretability

Reverse-engineering the internal representations of large neural models to understand how computation is distributed across layers.

Mechanistic Interpretability Circuit Analysis
